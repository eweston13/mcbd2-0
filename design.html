Subsystems

At the start of our design process, the team broke down the MCBD into three components: the outer spherical wheel, the driving yoke, and an internal structure to maintain magnetic coupling between the wheel and the yoke. We then generated several subsystems concepts and produced design concepts from various combinations of the subsystem concepts.

Final Design & Technical Analysis

Actuator Configuration

Our final design eliminated the need for an internal structure by adding ferritic rods to the spherical wheel itself. We determined that for our intended use, each ball module would only need to actuate in one direction, as we expect them to be implemented in groups of three or four. Holonomic motion and rotational motion can be achieved by installing multiple modules as shown below. However, each module will have two omni-chains, to help center the spherical wheel within the yoke and allow for more consistent control.

Spherical Wheel Design

The final spherical wheel design is two thick-walled, bolt-filled, polypropylene copolymer hemispheres that will screw together. The hemispheres will be 1-inch thick, as determined by a static load analysis performed in Solidworks. The material was chosen for its cheap cost, low density, and relatively high tensile strength. The bolts threaded through the wheel will act as coupling components with the yoke. An outer coating of nitrile rubber will be applied to the outer surface of the wheel after assembly to increase the ball drive's traction on the ground. The coating will add relatively little mass to the wheel, and we expect it to be easy to obtain.

Magnet Arrays

After conducting a magnetic field calculation, the team determined that mounting disc magnets to the omni-chains would be insufficient to maintain coupling within the ball drive. To compensate for this, the team has added additional magnets along the perimeter and top of the yoke. The top snap-fit enclosures for the magnets are a simple array that will allow the team to rearrange magnets freely without needing to refabricate the yoke.

Omni-Chain Links

With the addition of a second omni-chain, the team investigated the contact between the chains and the spherical wheel. We found that a flat omni-link would not have enough surface contact with the wheel, so the omni-chain links have been changed to more closely match the surface of the spherical wheel.
